Understanding Forbes Billionaires by Visualizations

Welcome to the visualization of Forbes Billionaires Ranking!

As we slowly stepping out the shadow of the COVID-19 pandemic, the global economy starts to show signs of recovery, while still lingering with challenges. One industry that has experienced a significant impact is the IT sector, which has witnessed widespread layoffs and downsizing measures. Unfortunately, other industries have also faced uncertain prospects during these times. It is against this backdrop that the release of Forbes' billionaires ranking for 2023 in April becomes particularly intriguing. This prestigious list not only highlights the extraordinary wealth amassed by individuals but also serves as a barometer for the state of the economy. The fluctuation in rankings can provide valuable insights into the economic landscape, revealing the effects of the recent turbulence and offering a glimpse into the resilience and adaptability of the world's wealthiest individuals. Join us as we explore this captivating visualization, delving into the correlation between billionaire rankings and the ever-evolving economic conditions.

On this web page, I offer you a remarkable journey through a vast sea of data, transforming the traditionally mundane ranking table into a captivating visual experience. With thousands of rows scraped from Forbes' official website using Python, I have aggregated and transformed the data into visually engaging representations that provide a comprehensive understanding of this ranking. Through various types of visualizations, I offer you a fresh perspective on the world of billionaires. My aim is to present a holistic view of the rankings, going beyond mere numbers to unravel the captivating stories behind the ranking. Join us as we delve into these visualizations, exploring different dimensions and uncovering patterns, trends, and outliers. Gain a deeper understanding of the dynamics of wealth distribution, industry dominance, geographical representation, and more. Prepare to be mesmerized by the power of data visualization as we unravel the intriguing stories behind the rankings and shed light on the captivating world of billionaires.

Comparing Top 20 Billionaires in 2022 and 2023

The world's richest person in 2022 is Elon Musk with 219 billion USD, but in April 2023, it has become Bernard Arnault with 211 billion USD. Elon's wealth shrinks to 180 billion USD. This decline not only happen to Elon alone, but also Jeff Bezos, Warren Buffett, Bill Gates, etc as you can compare in the bar chart. The shrink of wealth for IT billionaires corresponds with the general depression in IT industry.

Knowing wealth of billionaires summed by each country. People starts to be curious about what industry does those billionaires belongs to. What are their source that helps them make money?
In this stacked bar chart, you can find out, in each country, what are the summed wealth of billionaires for each industry.

As demonstrate in the graph, Finance and Technology are the two industries with highest proportion of billionaire's total wealth in U.S. This means in U.S. one major group of the most richest group of people make their money by Finance and Technology. And these two are the best field to make the most money. This reflects the economic structure of U.S. Like Finance and Technology to U.S.; for China, it is Manufacturing and Technnology; for France, it is Fashion; For Russia, it is Energy and Mining. We can surely say that the composition of billionaires in each country highly resonate with the country's economic strength.

Connection Between Billionaires

This node link diagram shows the connection between top 20 world billionaires in 2023 ranking. Color of node identify their countries. Size of node shows their net wealth. If two billionaires work in same industry, they will be connected by edges. The thickness of edges demonstrate the strength of connection. If two billionaires work in same industry and they come from the same country, their connection will be stronger.
